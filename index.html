Car Insurance

Car insurance is a type of motor policy that protect you from financial loss occurred due to damages such as theft, third-party damage, man-made and natural calamities. With valid car insurance you can get financial and legal protection against the damage caused by your car. Comprehensive insurance policy for car provides protection against damage caused to your own car. As per the Motor Vehicles Act of 1988, if you own a car, it is compulsory to have at least third-party car insurance to cover damage(s) caused to others.

What Is Car Insurance?

Car Insurance, is a legal agreement between a car owner and an insurance company. The insurance company promises to cover the costs if any damage occurs to your car that could lead to financial loss.

There are three different types of car insurance policies that have different levels of coverage: Third-Party Car Insurance, Standalone Own-Damage (OD) Car Insurance, and Comprehensive Car Insurance.

It’s important to renew your car insurance policy on time so you can keep enjoying the benefits of your chosen coverage.

Why do you need Car Insurance?

Car insurance provides several benefits to policyholders. Let’s look at some of the top benefits of buying car insurance online

Keeps you in-accordance with the law

Car insurance helps you comply with the law as it is mandatory for car owners in India to have at least third-party car insurance. Failure to comply can result in a fine of ₹2,000 and/or imprisonment for up to 3 months.

Financial protection against Losses

A car insurance policy can help you save money by covering huge expenses incurred due to damages and losses caused during accidents, natural calamities, fire, theft and other unforeseen circumstances. This can also help you avoid hefty traffic fines.

Protection against third-party liabilities

In case you accidentally hit someone, a car, or someone’s property, your car insurance will cover the damages and losses incurred to the third-party. The compensation for third-party property damage is limited but there is no compensation cap in case of third-party bodily injuries, disability or death.

Own-damage protection

Car insurance also covers the own-damage expenses of your car incurred in road accidents, natural calamities, man-made disasters, fire, explosion, vandalism, etc.

Coverage against theft

Car insurance also covers you in case your car gets stolen. Your insurance company provides you with your car’s Insured Declared Value (IDV).

Personal accident cover

Car insurance also offers personal accident cover of up to ₹15 Lakh in case of death, bodily injuries, or disability of the policyholder in a car accident. You can avail this add-on coverage by paying extra premium.

Additional coverage with add-ons

With a comprehensive car insurance policy, you can add extra coverage and benefits using add-ons like zero-depreciation cover, return to invoice cover, consumables cover, and breakdown assistance, amongst others.

What are the add-on covers in Car Insurance policy?

Add-on covers are extra benefits that you can add to your car or four-wheeler insurance policy to increase your protection. You can buy it with a comprehensive car insurance and Standalone OD Car insurance policy Here are some of the options you can choose from.

  1. No claim bonus protection:

    This allows you to file a certain number of car insurance claims while keeping your No Claim Bonus discount. It is only available with comprehensive car insurance.

  2. Roadside assistance

    This provides 24/7 breakdown assistance for your car if it breaks down in a remote location. Assistance including towing, fueling, and mechanic services is provided.

  3. Repair of glass fiber and plastic parts

    This add-on allows you to repair damaged glass, fiber, and plastic parts without affecting your No Claim Bonus discount. This add-on allows you to repair damaged glass, fiber, and plastic parts at network garage without affecting your No Claim Bonus discount at the time of renewal.

  4. Daily allowance

    aily Allowance Add-on provides a daily allowance to the policyholders in case the car is in the workshop for more than two days. In India, the amount of cash allowances generally provided under this add-on is of ₹500 for up to 14 days. The amount of compensation and the number of days can vary across insurance companies.

  5. Emergency transport and hotel expenses

    This covers your overnight accommodation expenses in a hotel and travel expenses if you are unable to reach your home/ destination due to an accident.

  6. Key replacement

    This covers the cost of repairing or replacing your car keys and locks if they are damaged or lost.

  7. Engine protection cover

    One of the beneficial riders is the Engine Secure cover, which safeguards one of your car’s most valuable parts. It covers the cost of repairing or replacing your engine in case of damage, especially in flood-prone areas.

  8. Tyre secure

    Covers the cost of repairing or replacing damaged tires or tubes in case of an accident.

  9. Consumables expenses

    Covers the cost of replacing consumables in your car that are lost or damaged due to an accident. Consumables include items like oil for the gearbox, air conditioner gas and nut and bolts. However, this add-on will not pay for fuel.

  10. Loss of personal belongings

    In case of an accident, the add-on covers the loss or damage of personal belongings such as clothes, video and/or audio tapes, and so on. The add-on does not provide coverage for the loss or damage of items like gold, cash, watches, debit and credit cards, or other valuables

  11. Return to invoice

    If you have purchased the Return to Invoice add-on, and your car is completely damaged or stolen, the insurance company will help cover the difference between the purchase price and the amount of compensation you receive (due to the depreciation). Some insurers may also cover the registration charges and road tax you have spent on the car.

  12. Zero depreciation cover (Depreciation Reimbursement)

    The zero depreciation or nil depreciation (Depreciation Reimbursement) add-on allows you to recover the deducted cost of depreciation on your car’s parts when you make a claim for damages. This means that you can receive up to the full sum insured without any reduction due to depreciation. It’s worth noting, however, the number of claims admissible under this add-on varies depending upon the insurance company and is applicable only to your own-damage car insurance policy or comprehensive car insurance policy.

  13. Personal accident cover for passengers

    This covers the passengers in your car in case of accidents leading to permanent disability or death.

How Is Car Insurance Premium Calculated?

The premium of third-party car insurance plans is regulated by IRDAI, while the premium for standalone own-damage and comprehensive plans varies across insurance companies. Below are some of the factors that one must consider.

Insured Declared Value (IDV)

The IDV is the maximum sum insured of the four-wheeler, that is the amount the insurance company pays the policyholder in case of total loss. Higher IDV attracts a higher premium.

Engine cubic capacity

The premium for a car insurance plan depends on the car’s engine cubic capacity. A higher cubic capacity means a higher premium.

Age of the vehicle

As the car ages, its value decreases due to normal wear and tear, which reduces its market value and, in turn, its insurance premium. Thus, the premium will be generally lower due to reduced IDV, and no claim made in previous policy.

Make, Model, and Variant

The car’s make, model, and variant affect its IDV, which, in turn, influences the car insurance premium. Also, premiums vary due to discounts by insurance companies depending on make, model and variant.

RTO location

One of the most important factors in determining the premium. It gives insurance companies an idea about the traffic density and the probability of road accidents and other mishaps of particular area. Note that the motor tariff rates also depend upon the “zone” under which the RTO falls.

How to Reduce Car Insurance Premium?

While car insurance provides financial protection against unforeseen accidents and damages, the premiums can sometimes feel burdensome. However, with the below mentioned strategies you can reduce your car insurance premium without compromising on coverage.

Compare policies

Before selecting an insurance policy, it’s essential to compare offerings from different insurance providers. Look for policies that provide comprehensive coverage at competitive rates. Online comparison tools and websites can be incredibly helpful in this regard.

Opt for voluntary deductibles

Voluntary deductibles involve agreeing to pay a certain portion of the repair costs in case of an accident. By choosing a higher voluntary deductible, you can lower your premium amount. However, make sure you can afford the deductible amount in case of a claim.

Install security features

Equipping your car with security features such as anti-theft alarms, immobilizers, and GPS tracking devices can make your vehicle less prone to theft. Insurance companies often offer discounts for such safety measures.

Maintain a good driving record

Insurance companies reward safe driving habits. Avoiding accidents and traffic violations over time can lead to a “no-claim bonus” (NCB) that significantly reduces your premium when you renew your policy.

Drive less

Some insurance providers offer “pay-as-you-drive” policies, where premiums are based on the distance you drive. If you don’t use your car extensively, this could be a cost-effective option.

Renew timely

Timely renewal of your policy is crucial to maintaining the accrued benefits like NCB. Failing to renew on time can result in the loss of these benefits.

Choose the right coverage

Assess your needs and select coverage that suits your requirements. While comprehensive coverage offers extensive protection, opting for third-party coverage might be more economical if your car is older.

Avoid small claims

For minor damages that can be managed without insurance claims, consider paying out of pocket. Frequent small claims can lead to higher premiums in the long run and affects your No claim bonus.

Check insured declared value

The Insured Declared Value (IDV) of your car is its current market value, which is important when getting car insurance as it determines the amount you will be insured for. In case of a total loss due to damage or theft, the insurance company will pay you the IDV when settling your claim. It’s crucial to choose the IDV carefully because a lower IDV may mean inadequate compensation in case of a total loss, even though it may result in lower premiums. On the other hand, a higher IDV may mean more expensive premiums.

If your car has minor or major damage, you can take it to one of your insurance provider’s approved garages for repairs. This way, you can take advantage of cashless claims, where the insurer will pay the repair costs directly to the garage, saving you from having to spend a lot of money on repairs. Hence, it is important to check that there is a strong network of cashless garages near you.

6

When you’re buying car insurance, it’s important to consider the claim settlement ratio of the insurance company. A high ratio means that you’re more likely to have your claims settled promptly. Additionally, the level of service provided by the company can impact how loyal you feel as a customer, so it’s worth checking before you choose a four-wheeler insurance policy.

How to File a Car Insurance Claim?

It’s important to know the right steps to take when making a car insurance claim, as your insurer may reject your claim for various reasons. Follow the steps below to make a valid four-wheeler insurance claim.

Step 1

Report the claim

Inform your insurer immediately after the accident and provide proper documentation and photographic evidence to support your claim.

Step 2

Inspection

Once the claim is registered, an insurer’s surveyor will inspect the claimed damage, and you’ll need to provide the necessary documents for claim verification.

Step 3

Repair

After inspection, you can send your car to a network garage for repairs.

Step 4

Settlement

If you have a cashless claim, the insurer will directly settle the repair bills with the network garage. However, if you have a reimbursement claim, you’ll need to pay the bills first and then make a reimbursement claim.

Documents Required to File a Car Insurance Claim

Here are some documents you might need to file a car insurance claim. Keep in mind that the list of required documents could differ based on your insurance company and the type of claim.

  1. Driver’s license

  2. Car insurance policy

  3. Registration certificate of the car policy

  4. Original car key

  5. Non-traceable Certificate (if necessary)

  6. Policyholder’s identification proof

  7. Fire brigade report (if applicable)

  8. Original purchase invoice of the car (if Return to Invoice add-on cover is chosen)

  9. Original repair bills

  10. Original payment receipts
